= NonMetals (Has a Relatively Low Density and High Electronegativity; Range from Colorless Gases to Shiny Solids; Poor Conductors)
= Metalloids (Has a Preponderance of Properties in between those of Metals and NonMetals; Have a Metallic Appearance, but are Brittle and only fair Conductors of Electricity)
= Transition Metals (Lustrous and Have Good Electrical and Thermal Conductivity; Most are Hard and Strong; Have High Melting and Boiling Points)
= Post-Transition Metals (Are Soft (or Brittle); Have Poor Mechanical Strength; Usually Have Melting Points Lower than those of the Transition Metals)
= Alkali Metals (Are Shiny, Soft, Highly Reactive Metals at Standard Temperature and Pressure)
= Alkali Earth Metals (Are Shiny, Silvery-White, Somewhat Reactive Metals at Standard Temperature and Pressure)
= Noble Gases (Under Standard Conditions, they are all Odorless, Colorless, Monatomic Gases with Very Low Chemical Reactivity)
= Halogens (Means "Salt Former" or "Salt Maker"; When they React with Metals, they Produce a Wide Range of Salts)
= Lanthanides (Comprises the 15 Metallic Chemical Elements with Atomic Numbers 57-71; Collectively Known as the Rare-Earth Elements or Rare-Earth Metals)
= Actinides (Encompasses the 15 Metallic Chemical Elements with Atomic Numbers 89-103; Are Radioactive and Release Energy upon Radioactive Decay)
